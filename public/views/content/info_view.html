What is Circular Economy?

It is an economic model that considers a growing demand for products and a decrease in raw materials, in which it is essential to find a way to reuse the waste of a product once it has served its purpose. This model seeks to extend the product's life cycle and reduce the negative impact on the environment.

Its antitheses are the linear economic model and planned obsolescence. The linear model is that when a product fulfills its function, its waste is not reusable, and that such waste is usually not environmentally friendly. Planned obsolescence is when, although a product may have a long life cycle, it is intentionally shortened in order for the consumer to consume more in a shorter period of time, which means a greater economic benefit for the producer, at the cost of abusing the product's life cycle. producer, at the cost of inconsiderately abusing existing raw materials.

Game instructions

1. One team produces will produce 3 - 5 fruits. To make fruit you need a set of ingredients. ingredients. You can produce for the domestic market or for export, with organic or normal quality to sell at different prices. Different markets and types of fruits require different quality of ingredients. Different ingredients have different prices.

2. Ingredients:

  • Chemical A
  • Chemical B
  • Organic A1
  • Organic A2
  • Organic B2
  • Organic B2
  • Manufacturer A
  • Manufacturer B
  • Water
  • Energy
  • Earth

3. In order to produce a fruit, the following is required:

Chemical + Organic#1 + Organic#2 + Manufacturer + Water / Energy / Earth

4. Some ingredients can be obtained based on:

  • Chemical A = Organic A1 + Orgnic A2
  • Chemical B = Organic B1 + Orgnic B2
  • Organic A1 = Organic B1
  • Organic A2 = Organic B2

5. Market rules:

  • Fruits created with ingredients A can be exported
  • Fruits created with ingredients B can NOT be exported, only national market
  • Ingredients A and B can be mixed, but the product will not be able to be exported

6. You can buy as many ingredients as you want each round, or you can keep stock for future rounds

7. The bank's inventory is limited

8. Penalty for excess ingredients at the end of the fifth round: double the purchase price

9. You can donate ingredients to other teams to avoid penalties
